Recent Projects

Leo Rover Group Project

Goal: The project aimed to build an autonomous robot that could explore unknown areas, recognize and sort objects, and operate safely under time and system constraints.
My Contribution: I contributed to hardware-software integration, including SLAMTEC LiDAR setup, Intel NUC configuration, and ROS-based control development.
What I Learned: I gained hands-on experience in real-time robotics, sensor integration, and the practical challenges of building autonomous systems.

A research on robotic systems for sustainable development

Goal: The project explored how robotics can support the UN Sustainable Development Goals across sectors like healthcare and agriculture.
My Contribution: I analyzed real-world case studies and evaluated ethical concerns like privacy and job loss to promote responsible robotics.
What I Learned: I learned how robotics and sustainability intersect, and gained skills in research, critical thinking, and ethical evaluation.

Characterisation of the RPLIDAR A2M12 for Autonomous Navigationt

Goal: The project evaluated the RPLIDAR A2M12's performance on the Leo Rover by analyzing range, resolution, and surface effects.
My Contribution: I conducted experiments using ROS2 to collect LIDAR data, tested surface effects on detection, and ensured data consistency and documentation.
What I Learned: I gained practical skills in LIDAR sensing, data analysis, and understanding how sensor properties impact autonomous navigation.

A Path Planning Algorithm for Autonomous Mobile Robots

Goal: The project aimed to implement the A* algorithm for robot path planning in a GUI-based simulation.
My Contribution: I implemented the A* algorithm in Python from scratch, enabling grid-based pathfinding with obstacle avoidance.
What I Learned: I deepened my understanding of A*, improved debugging and optimization skills, and learned the value of clean code in GUI-integrated systems.

Op-amp analysis, measurement, and design

Goal: The project aimed to deepen understanding of op-amps through analysis, simulation, and performance-focused circuit design.
My Contribution: I performed theoretical analysis, simulated op-amp circuits in NI Multisim, and designed advanced configurations to enhance performance.
What I Learned: I improved my skills in analog circuit design and simulation, and learned how design choices affect electronic system behavior.

Computer Cluster and Campus Grid project

Goal: The project explored cloud-based high-performance computing using a virtual cluster with focus on parallelism and scalability.
My Contribution: I deployed an Azure-based virtual cluster, ran MPI programs for parallel processing, and benchmarked performance against another setup.
What I Learned: I learned practical cloud and parallel computing skills, and how to analyze distributed system performance based on real data.

Antenna design and simulation

Goal: The project focused on designing and simulating dipole and Yagi-Uda antennas to optimize their performance through structural tuning.
My Contribution: I optimized a dipole antenna, then designed a Yagi-Uda antenna with enhanced directionality, achieving a peak gain of 11.1934 dBi.
What I Learned: I gained practical skills in antenna design and simulation, and a strong grasp of RF performance metrics like gain and impedance.

University Project (Electronic Design, Manufacture & Test)

Goal: The project involved creating a 3×3×3 LED cube responsive to wireless commands and ambient sound for interactive visual effects.
My Contribution: I helped design, build, and program the LED cube, integrating wireless control and sound responsiveness using a microphone.
What I Learned: I enhanced my skills in electronics, microcontroller programming, teamwork, and real-time system troubleshooting.
